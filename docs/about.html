I moved to Stockholm in early 2016 with an extra dose of optimism. My plan was to begin my design career by convincing a Swedish company to hire me and sponsor my work visa. The fact that I had no professional design experience didn’t enter my mind as a credible threat to my plan.

Fortune favors the bold, or so they say. Five weeks after I arrived in Sweden, I had an offer from

Designit
for a 6-month paid internship. I love design and haven’t looked back since.

Many people I meet here ask me about my story and how I ended up in Sweden. I’ve lost track of how many times I’ve been asked, how could you leave NYC?! Below is a brief visual history.

Hi.

March 2016. About a month before moving to Sweden. A lot on my mind.

I was born and raised in Brooklyn, NY. Until moving to Sweden, I had spent 99% of my life either in Brooklyn or within a 2-hour drive.

My family is important to me. But since none of them are designers, they won’t really be interested in reading my site unless I tell them I sprinkled in some pics of them :)

Yep, that’s some of my peoples. Not many pics before the smart phone era (can you relate?) so most are relatively recent.

I attended middle and high school at Poly Prep, an amzing place in Brooklyn.

Wrestling teammates for 7 years!

A few months after high school, my family drove me down I-95 to the University of Pennsylvania, where I spent the next 4 years.

This is a beautiful picture of a highway in Iceland. There’s nothing scenic about
driving through New Jersey so I spared you that visual.

A few too many drinks, parties, and all-nighters later, I graduated with a Political Science degree.

Two of my bros at graduation. Sorry for being a stranger, fellas.

Like apparently many of my peers, I felt unsettled with what I was doing in life for a while. My first job out of university was on Wall St but I was bored out of my mind. Eventually, the fashion world discovered me and I modeled for several years.

Fitting room glamour. Tommy Hilfiger suit.

The life of a model involves many castings and trying to stay busy while you wait for some good news from your agent. It’s funny that, after all of those years, I booked my two most notable jobs in the same week. One page in GQ and 3 pages in Men’s Health.

  • Happier than I look!

I am most grateful to modeling for affording me the time to find my passion. I tried a few tech industry specialties, but when I found design, I knew instantly that my search ended. A few months later, I was in the UX Immersive program at General Assembly. Dollar for dollar, the most value I’ve gotten from education.

My graduating class with our teachers. Thank you to my official and unofficial teachers: Nevan Scott,
Rashida White, Tricia Davis, Jess Eddy, Domenick Propati, Luke Miller.

After the immersive, I had the skills to solve simple design problems. A Brazilian friend named Helder reached out for help designing Pitaco, his Rio de Janeiro based startup. I enlisted one of my design school mates, Pedro do Ó. Our work was in the App Store a few months later.

The Brazilian football (soccer) social
betting app that we designed. It was in
the App Store for about a year.

Eventually, I decided it was time to go explore the warmth of other suns (to steal a cool phrase from a great book) and leave the distractions of NYC. I received my family’s cautious blessing and set sail for Stockholm- light on the luggage, heavy on the ambition.

My bro walked me to the subway on my way to the airport.

Designit was a great learning experience. It’s an international agency with many talented designers.

For my first Designit project, I got thrown into the fire. I came off the bench, mid-project, for 2 designers who left for a week-long conference. Talk about intensity.

Mid-way through my internship, I spent a month in Designit’s London office. I worked with the team to redesign the digital experience for Chelsea FC.

I felt out of place at first because I thought these guys were part of the original Dream Team… good times!

It has been over a year since changing countries and careers and, boy, what a ride. I never anticipated quite how challenging and rewarding it would be. I’m on my 2nd job as a full-time designer and I am enjoying every second. I’ve come a long way and there’s so much more to learn!

When I’m not designing, I spend time with my and any of our friends in the city.
